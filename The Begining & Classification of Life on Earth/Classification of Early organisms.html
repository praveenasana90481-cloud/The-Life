The Classification of Organisms

Everything you need to know about the five main groups of living organisms

Monera

Ancient Bacteria (Archaebacteria)

The oldest living things. They can live in very hot or salty places.

True Bacteria (Eubacteria)

Common bacteria found everywhere, like in soil or on your skin.

Blue-Green Algae (Cyanobacteria)

Bacteria that can make food from sunlight like plants.

Tiny Bacteria (Mycoplasma)

The smallest cells ever found. They don't have a cell wall.

Fungus-like Bacteria (Actinomycetes)

Bacteria that grow like threads. We use them to make medicine.

Hitchhiker Bacteria (Rickettsia)

Very small bacteria that must live inside the cells of other animals.

Protista

Golden Algae (Chrysophytes)

Tiny floating things in the ocean with glass-like shells.

Spinning Algae (Dinoflagellates)

Ocean creatures with two tails that help them spin around.

Mixer Cells (Euglenoids)

They act like plants in the light and like animals in the dark.

Crawling Cells (Amoeboid)

They move by changing their shape and stretching out.

Tail-Waggers (Flagellated)

They use a long whip-like tail to swim through water.

Hairy Cells (Ciliated)

They are covered in tiny hairs that help them swim fast.

Spore-Makers (Sporozoans)

Tiny parasites that can cause sickness, like Malaria.

Slime Molds

Brightly colored blobs that eat rotting wood and leaves.

Water Molds

Fuzzy growths that live in water or very wet soil.

Fungi

Algae-like Fungi (Phycomycetes)

Simple molds often found on damp bread or in water.

Sac Fungi (Ascomycota)

Fungi that look like tiny cups or bags. Includes yeast.

Club Fungi (Basidiomycota)

The typical mushrooms we see in the woods or on pizza.

Imperfect Fungi (Deuteromycota)

Fungi that don't fit in other groups. Many cause skin rashes.

Root Partners (Glomeromycota)

Special fungi that live in plant roots and help them grow.

Plantae

Simple Algae (Thallophyta)

Plants without roots or stems that live in water.

Mosses (Bryophyta)

Soft, green plants that grow in damp places and have no seeds.

Ferns (Pteridophyta)

Plants with leaves that use spores instead of seeds to grow.

Cone Plants (Gymnosperms)

Trees that have "naked" seeds in cones, like Pine trees.

Flower Plants (Angiosperms)

Plants that make flowers and keep their seeds inside fruit.

Animalia

Animals without Backbones (Invertebrates)

Includes insects, spiders, worms, and jellyfish.

Animals with Backbones (Vertebrates)

Includes fish, frogs, snakes, birds, and humans.
