TISSUES

Tissues are groups of similar cells that work together to carry out a specific function. They form the basic structural and functional framework in both plants and animals, helping in growth, support, transport, movement, and coordination. In essence, tissues act as the link between individual cells and larger organs or systems.

Classification of Tissues

Tissues are groups of cells that work together to perform a specific job. Broadly, they are categorized into plant tissues and animal tissues.

Plant Tissues

Plant tissues help in growth, transport of water and nutrients, and provide support. They enable plants to stand upright, distribute food and water throughout their structure, and continue growing throughout their life.

Animal Tissues

Animal tissues form organs and systems, enabling movement, protection, communication, and coordination. They allow animals to respond quickly to their environment, move freely, and maintain internal balance.

General Characteristics

  • Cellular Grouping: Tissues are formed by clusters of similar cells that share structural features.
  • Common Purpose: All cells in a tissue coordinate to perform a specialized function such as support, transport, movement, or communication.
  • Origin: Cells within a tissue usually arise from the same embryonic or developmental origin, ensuring uniformity in their role.
  • Arrangement: Cells are organized in specific patterns (layers, bundles, or networks) that suit their function and efficiency.
  • Interdependence: Tissues interact with other tissues to form organs and organ systems, working together for overall function.
  • Adaptability: Depending on the organism, tissues can adapt structurally (e.g., thickened walls in plants, muscle growth in animals) to meet environmental or functional demands.

Differences

Feature Plant Tissues Animal Tissues
Support Provide rigidity and strength to keep the plant upright in one place. Provide flexibility and elasticity, allowing animals to move freely.
Living Status Often include many dead cells (like xylem) that add strength and durability. Almost all cells are living, actively participating in body functions.
Growth Growth is localized at specific regions (tips of roots and shoots). Growth occurs more uniformly across tissues throughout the body.
Energy Require less energy since plants remain stationary. Require more energy to support movement and active processes.
Structure Organization is relatively simple and repetitive, focused on support and transport. Organization is complex, forming organs and systems for specialized functions.
Coordination Functions are mainly regulated by hormones and environmental factors. Functions are coordinated by both the nervous system and hormones.
